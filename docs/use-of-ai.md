# Use of AI in Project Development  
Institutional Analytics Model

This document outlines how AI tools were used during the development of this project.


## Role of AI

AI tools were used as assistive development aids to support:

- Drafting documentation structure  
- Refining technical explanations  
- Improving clarity and formatting  
- Generating example schema visualisations (e.g., Mermaid diagrams)  

AI was not used to automatically generate business logic, fabricate data relationships, or replace analytical reasoning.

## Human Oversight & Validation

All outputs generated with AI assistance were:

- Reviewed and validated manually  
- Adjusted to reflect accurate modelling decisions  
- Checked against BI best practices  
- Aligned with higher education governance expectations  

Data model design, grain definition, surrogate key strategy, KPI logic, and security architecture were determined through deliberate modelling decisions.


## AI & Ethical Considerations

The project intentionally avoids:

- Use of real student PII  
- Automated decision-making affecting individuals  
- Unvalidated predictive outputs  

Where risk scores are referenced, they are illustrative features within a synthetic environment and do not represent deployed institutional decision systems.


## Responsible AI Principles Applied

This project follows responsible AI principles including:

- Transparency of AI involvement  
- Clear documentation of modelling assumptions  
- Human review and accountability  
- Governance-first design  

AI was treated as a productivity enhancer rather than a decision authority.



## Summary

AI tools were used to support efficiency in documentation and diagram generation. Core architectural, modelling, governance, and analytical decisions remain human-led and context-driven.

The inclusion of this section reflects a commitment to transparency and responsible technology use within institutional analytics environments.
